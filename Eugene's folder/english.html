The National Video Game Museum

The National Videogame Museum is the place to be for anyone who wants to know more about the history, social and cultural cultural side of video games. The museum also lets its visitors experience the games of tomorrow. Gamers from young to old are welcome to play, discover, learn and share experiences online.

Our history

Here are our story

A garage box in The Hague is where the adventure began in 2008, with two friends Hasan Tasdemir and Pascal Rappailles. The garage box soon became too small after which they moved to a shed in an industrial area. Unfortunately, the shed was not heated and everything had to be moved to an empty Ministry of Agriculture building in the middle of winter. When this building also had to be vacated, the then collection of 10 arcade video games with some spare parts was stored in an old classic car showroom. Since there was no room there to renovate or play the cabinets, suitable space was sought. That was found in 2011 at the Cobaltstraat in Zoetermeer. There Hasan decided to rent the location with 4 friends and the adventure could grow into its own meeting place to practice their hobby and relax with a game.

Soon there were more people who wanted to participate, which meant that both the location and the hobby approach were no longer adequate in addition to everyone's daily work. The initiative was in danger of succumbing to its success. It was therefore decided by the friends that a professional approach was necessary and it could no longer be continued on a hobby basis. By mutual agreement, it was decided that one of the friends, Hasan Tasdemir, would try to develop the concept into a business. In which the others would support him with advice and assistance in their spare time. Hasan decided to leave Cobaltstraat in 2014 and move to Bleiswijkseweg. Since income was still marginal, only anti-squatting properties could be rented, which still required frequent moves. From Stephensonstraat to Edisonstraat where beautiful events under the name Retro Planet were organized for the community. The collection also continued to expand. While the interest in retro gaming from family and friends grew bigger and bigger. For that reason, in 2015 Hasan took the important step of bringing his now extensive collection into a professional company called Playworks BV.​

In 2016, Hasan came into contact with Jan Kragt, city marketer for the municipality of Zoetermeer, who, deeply impressed by the collection, suggested the idea of turning it into a museum and setting up a foundation for that purpose. On the spot, Hasan and Jan came up with the name and the National Videogame Museum was born. Together with a number of important players from the Dutch game community, the National Videogame Museum Foundation was then created. The city council was enthusiastic about the idea and decided to support the museum financially. This made it possible to rent a heated storage facility where the entire collection could be housed and partially exhibited. In early 2017, in close cooperation with the municipality, a real museum location was found: the former V&D building in the city center of Zoetermeer.

Starting in May 2017, a large group of volunteers and fans worked extremely hard to get the pilot of the museum set up so that it was ready to play on December 1, 2017! The huge influx of enthusiastic visitors and the many positive comments about our museum speak for themselves. The National Video Game Museum is now a huge success, thanks in part to the tireless efforts of all our staff and volunteers.

Meanwhile, we are busy expanding the museum, deepening the experience and enhancing the museological and educational value of our beautiful museum. We are doing everything we can to further professionalize the museum and make it ready for the next "level" in a permanent and definitive location. And our ambition goes much further, because we want the National Videogame Museum to grow into a total concept, including:

  • A National Game Knowledge Center;
  • A National Video Game Archive;
  • an eSports arena;
  • A Hacker/makerspace for young and old;
  • a game fair and event venue
